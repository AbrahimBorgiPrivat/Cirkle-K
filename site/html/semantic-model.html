Article 03 / Semantic model

Semantic Model

The interview case uses a compact star schema. The simulation case expands that idea into a wider loyalty model with customer, campaign, and transaction entities.

The interview document explicitly describes a central transaction fact linked to date, item, and site dimensions. The simulation document adds customers, cards, campaigns, stations, cashiers, and transaction lines.
Interview semantic model

Simple star schema

The reporting model centers on `TRANSACTIONS`, with supporting dimensions for date, item, and site to support year-over-year, product, and geography analysis.

Simulation semantic model

Wider loyalty structure

The simulation model adds customers, cards, campaigns, stations, cashiers, and transaction lines to support segmentation and campaign analysis.

Interview entities

  • `TRANSACTIONS` as the central fact table.
  • `DATE_DIM` for SPLY and period slicing.
  • `ITEM_MASTER` for product names, categories, and images.
  • `SITE_MASTER` for region, city, and station context.

Simulation entities

  • `LOYALITY_CUSTOMERS`, `CARDS`, and `SEGMENTATIONSGROUPS` describe the loyalty side.
  • `TRANSACTIONS` and `TRANSACTIONSLINES` capture purchase behavior.
  • `CAMPAIGNS` and `CAMPAIGN_TRANSACTIONS` support redemption analysis.
  • `STATIONS` and `CASHIERS` connect behavior to geography and point of sale.
Why it matters

A clean semantic model keeps measures reusable, visuals easier to explain, and Tabular automation practical.
